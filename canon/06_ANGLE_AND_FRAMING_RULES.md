# 06_ANGLE_AND_FRAMING_RULES

**Status:** CANONICAL  
**Version:** v0.1

## Purpose
Governs how the system identifies, labels, and presents the "Angle" or narrative frame used by different sources.

## Hard Constraints
*   **Descriptive Framing:** Angles must be described using neutral, structural terms (e.g., "History-Focus," "Humanitarian-Focus," "Security-Focus").
*   **Evidence Linking:** Every determination of an "Angle" must include a link or citation to the specific text/headline element that justifies it.
*   **Forbidden Labels:** Terms implying malice or deception (e.g., "Pro-Terror," "Zionist Propaganda," "Hateful") are forbidden as system labels.
*   **Aggregation:** Angles are aggregated from the content (headline, lead paragraph, image choice), not assumed from the outlet's reputation.

## Explicit Non-Goals
*   **No Psychoanalysis:** We do not guess what the editor *intended* to make the reader feel.
*   **No Binary Buckets:** We avoid simple "Left vs. Right" labels where possible, preferring more specific descriptors relevant to the event.
